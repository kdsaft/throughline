The Pie That Conquered the Canopy
Deep within the heart of the jungle, a hierarchical society of
big-eyed birds thrived in the dense canopy.
Among these birds, an annual pie-making contest determined
the social standing of each family.
Those who ranked higher gained access to better
resources, education, and opportunities.
The competition was fierce, with families gathering
in the Great Banyan Clearing to present their pies.
The goal was to impress the judges and claim
the esteemed title of Pie Master, which granted
the winners the most luxurious nests on the highest branches.
